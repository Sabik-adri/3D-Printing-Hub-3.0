Resin 3D Printing (SLA)

Resin 3D printing, using Stereolithography (SLA), is a high-precision method for creating detailed three-dimensional objects with photopolymer resins. It is ideal for applications requiring fine details and smooth finishes.

Applications

  • Prototyping: High-detail prototypes for visual and functional testing.
  • Dental and Medical: Custom dental models, surgical guides, and prosthetics.
  • Jewelry: Intricate designs for investment casting.
  • Engineering: Functional parts and assemblies for testing and end-use.

Why Choose Our SLA 3D Printing Services?

  • Advantages:
    • High Detail and Precision: Produces intricate parts with fine details.
    • Smooth Surface Finish: Minimal layer lines for a polished look.
    • Wide Range of Materials: Various resins for diverse applications.

Materials

Standard Resin

Versatile for general-purpose applications with good detail and finish.

Tough Resin

Durable and wear-resistant, ideal for functional prototypes.

Flexible Resin

Elastic and flexible, suitable for parts requiring bendability.

High-Temperature Resin

Withstands high temperatures for thermal-resistant applications.

Castable Resin

Designed for investment casting, used in jewelry and dentistry.

Biocompatible Resin

Safe for medical and dental applications, meeting regulatory standards.

Considerations

  • Post-Processing: Requires cleaning and curing, adding to production time.
  • Material Cost: Resins are often more expensive than filament-based printing.
  • Brittleness: Some resins may be brittle, limiting certain applications.
