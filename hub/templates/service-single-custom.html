Custom 3D Printed Parts

Custom 3D printed parts offer a versatile solution for prototyping, production, and artistic applications, enabling tailored designs with rapid production and cost-effectiveness.

Applications

  • Prototyping: Create and test prototypes quickly to validate designs.
  • Production Parts: Manufacture custom components for industries like automotive, aerospace, and medical.
  • Replacement Parts: Produce hard-to-find or discontinued parts.
  • Artistic and Decorative Items: Create custom designs and sculptures.
  • Functional Components: Develop parts with specific functionalities, such as brackets, housings, or tools.

Why Choose Our Custom 3D Printed Parts Services?

  • Advantages:
    • Customization: Tailor parts to precise specifications and unique designs.
    • Speed: Rapid production and iteration reduce development times.
    • Cost-Effectiveness: Lower costs for short runs and prototypes.
    • Complex Geometries: Create intricate and complex designs with ease.

Materials

PLA (Polylactic Acid)

Biodegradable and cost-effective, suitable for prototypes and low-stress applications.

ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)

Strong and durable, ideal for functional and mechanical parts.

PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol)

Combines strength and flexibility, offering durability and ease of printing.

Nylon

Strong, flexible, and durable, suitable for mechanical parts and gears.

TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane)

Flexible and elastic, ideal for parts requiring impact resistance.

Resin

High detail and smooth finish, suitable for intricate and small parts.

Composite Materials

Carbon fiber or fiberglass reinforced for added strength and rigidity.

Metal Filaments

Infused with metal particles for strength and heat resistance.

Engineering-Grade Plastics

PEEK, PEI (Ultem), and others for high-performance applications.

Considerations

  • Material Properties: Choose materials based on strength, flexibility, durability, and temperature resistance.
  • Design Accuracy: Ensure designs are accurate and meet functional requirements.
  • Post-Processing: Some parts may require finishing for improved performance or aesthetics.
  • Cost vs. Quantity: Consider whether 3D printing is cost-effective for the required quantity.
