3D Printing Tolerance & Accuracy Guide

Understanding tolerance and accuracy in 3D printing is essential for producing parts that meet precise design requirements, ensuring fit and functionality.

Why Tolerance & Accuracy Matter

  • Precision: Achieve parts that closely match design specifications.
  • Fit and Function: Ensure components fit together correctly for assemblies.
  • Quality Control: Minimize errors and improve part reliability.
  • Cost Efficiency: Reduce rework by optimizing designs for printing accuracy.

Key Concepts

  • Tolerance:
    • Allowable variation in dimensions from the intended design.
    • Ensures parts remain functional within acceptable limits.
  • Accuracy:
    • How closely printed dimensions match the design.
    • Critical for high-precision applications.

Factors Affecting Tolerance & Accuracy

Printer Type and Technology

  • FDM/FFF: ±0.1 to ±0.5 mm tolerances.
  • SLA/DLP: ±0.05 to ±0.1 mm for high accuracy.
  • SLS: ±0.1 to ±0.2 mm with good repeatability.

Material Properties

  • Thermal Expansion: Materials like ABS may warp due to temperature changes.
  • Shrinkage: Thermoplastics shrink slightly after cooling.

Design Factors

  • Feature Size: Small details are prone to inaccuracies.
  • Wall Thickness: Thin walls may not print accurately.

Printing Parameters

  • Layer Height: Finer layers improve detail but increase print time.
  • Print Speed: Higher speeds may reduce accuracy.
  • Temperature: Proper settings prevent warping and adhesion issues.

Post-Processing

  • Sanding/Finishing: Improves tolerances and surface quality.
  • Assembly: Design tolerances to accommodate slight variations.

Tolerance Specifications

  • FDM/FFF: ±0.1 to ±0.5 mm, depending on printer and settings.
  • SLA/DLP: ±0.05 to ±0.1 mm for high-precision parts.
  • SLS: ±0.1 to ±0.2 mm with consistent results.

Tips for Improving Tolerance & Accuracy

  • Calibrate Printer: Ensure consistent performance.
  • Optimize Settings: Adjust layer height, speed, and temperature.
  • Choose Material: Select materials with stable properties.
  • Test and Iterate: Validate tolerances with test prints.

Examples of Tolerances for Common Applications

  • Interlocking Parts: Use 0.2-0.5 mm clearance for proper fit.
  • Functional Parts: Aim for ±0.1 mm tolerances for moving or load-bearing components.
  • Cosmetic Parts: ±0.5 mm tolerances are often sufficient for non-functional parts.

Conclusion

By understanding tolerance and accuracy factors, you can design and print parts that meet precise requirements. Contact us for specific guidance or support with your project.
