Explore the properties of common 3D printing plastics to choose the best material for your project, from prototyping to functional parts.

Why Use Material Data Sheets?

  • Informed Decisions: Understand material properties to select the right one for your application.
  • Optimize Printing: Use recommended settings for best print quality.
  • Ensure Safety: Follow safety and handling guidelines for each material.
  • Versatile Applications: Match materials to specific project needs, from prototypes to durable parts.

Accessing Material Data Sheets

For precise details, refer to Material Data Sheets from manufacturers like XYZ Printing, ABC Materials, and others. These can be found on their websites or obtained from suppliers.

  • Key Information:
    • Density, thermal properties, and print settings.
    • Chemical resistance and safety guidelines.
    • Applications for prototyping, functional, or outdoor use.
  • How to Access:
    • Visit manufacturer websites or contact suppliers.
    • Request specific data sheets for your material or brand.

Material Data Sheets

PLA (Polylactic Acid)

  • Manufacturer: XYZ Printing
  • Density: 1.24 g/cm³
  • Glass Transition Temp: 60°C
  • Melting Temp: 180-220°C
  • Print Temp: 190-220°C
  • Bed Temp: 0-60°C
  • Applications: Prototyping, educational models, decorative items

ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)

  • Manufacturer: ABC Materials
  • Density: 1.04 g/cm³
  • Glass Transition Temp: 105°C
  • Melting Temp: 220-250°C
  • Print Temp: 220-250°C
  • Bed Temp: 80-110°C
  • Applications: Functional parts, mechanical components, automotive parts

PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol)

  • Manufacturer: DEF Filaments
  • Density: 1.27 g/cm³
  • Glass Transition Temp: 80°C
  • Melting Temp: 230-250°C
  • Print Temp: 220-250°C
  • Bed Temp: 70-90°C
  • Applications: Functional prototypes, containers, mechanical parts

Nylon (Polyamide)

  • Manufacturer: GHI Polymers
  • Density: 1.14 g/cm³
  • Glass Transition Temp: 70°C
  • Melting Temp: 240-260°C
  • Print Temp: 240-270°C
  • Bed Temp: 70-100°C
  • Applications: Mechanical parts, gears, functional prototypes

TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane)

  • Manufacturer: JKL Filaments
  • Density: 1.20 g/cm³
  • Glass Transition Temp: -50°C
  • Melting Temp: 210-230°C
  • Print Temp: 210-230°C
  • Bed Temp: 30-60°C
  • Applications: Flexible parts, seals, gaskets, wearables

ASA (Acrylonitrile Styrene Acrylate)

  • Manufacturer: MNO Materials
  • Density: 1.06 g/cm³
  • Glass Transition Temp: 105°C
  • Melting Temp: 230-250°C
  • Print Temp: 230-250°C
  • Bed Temp: 80-100°C
  • Applications: Outdoor parts, automotive components, functional prototypes
